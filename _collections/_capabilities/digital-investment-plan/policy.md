Direct link: www.digital.gov.au/investment/strategic-planning/digital-investment-plans-dips/digital-investment-plan-policy Responsible agency: Digital Transformation Agency Last updated: 25 June 2025 The Digital Investment Plan (DIP) Policy and accompanying guidebook establishes a consistent, comprehensive and long-term approach to digital investment planning across government. It enables the Digital Transformation Agency (DTA) to effectively aggregate a whole-of-government view of the digital investment landscape. The policy was developed to address critical gaps in planning by establishing a structured approach to short, medium and long-term digital investment planning. The policy: specifies a consistent approach and minimum standards for strategic, future-focused digital investment planning supports agencies to align with the vision and missions of the Data and Digital Government Strategy and other strategic priorities. The guidebook: outlines minimum content requirements for each section of a DIP includes templates and guidance for content development. Applicability The policy applies to all non-corporate Commonwealth entities subject to the Digital and ICT Investment Oversight Framework (IOF). Access the policy The digital.gov.au website hosts the Digital Investment Plan (DIP) Policy .

## Policy requirements

### Requirement 1

Have a digital investment plan in place by 1 July 2025.

### Requirement 2

Designate a suitable responsible officer to approve the plan.

### Requirement 3

Submit an approved plan to the DTA at least once annually.

### Requirement 4

Address the following minimum content requirements outlined in the DIP Guidebook:

* an agency overview, introducing its role and functions
* a list of important current systems that the agency uses or provides
* a digital outlook vision statement
* a planning section containing the digital investment plan or a roadmap
* enablers that underpin the plan's delivery
* risks which might disrupt the plan's delivery.

### Requirement 5

Ensure that proposals, projects and ongoing digital initiatives assessed by the DTA as part of the IOF are aligned with their plan.

### Requirement 6

Ensure all internally funded digital initiatives that are critical to agency functions are outlined in their plan.

### Requirement 7

Align the plan with the agency's strategies and demonstrate contribution towards the Data and Digital Government Strategy.

### Requirement 8

Review and refine digital investment plans twice a year, in line with budget cycles.
