<p>Direct link: <a href="https://www.buyict.gov.au/sp?id=policy"><em>www.buyict.gov.au/sp?id=policy</em></a><br>Responsible agency: <strong>Digital Transformation Agency</strong><br>Last updated: <strong>31 March 2021</strong><br><br>The <em>Digital sourcing framework</em> is a set of principles, policies and guidance for government agencies buying digital products and services.&nbsp;</p><p>The framework promotes best practices to help agencies create a competitive and fair environment for digital sourcing and ensures procurement processes are transparent, efficient and effective.&nbsp;</p><h2>Applicability</h2><p>The <em>Digital sourcing framework</em> applies to non-corporate Commonwealth entities, as defined by the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.legislation.gov.au/Details/C2014C00317"><em>Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013</em></a>, and may be leveraged by other Commonwealth entities and companies.</p><h2>Access the framework</h2><p>The buyict.gov.au website hosts the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.buyict.gov.au/sp?id=policy"><em>Digital sourcing framework</em></a>&nbsp;(full text).</p><h2>Framework principles</h2><p>Agencies should follow the best-practice principles below when sourcing digital products and services:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>encourage competition</li><li>innovate and iterate often</li><li>enable small and medium enterprises (SMEs) to compete fairly</li><li>reuse components of other similar ICT projects</li><li>be outcome-focused</li><li>use open standards and cloud first</li><li>minimise cyber-security risks</li><li>avoid duplication by not building platforms other agencies have already built.</li></ul>
