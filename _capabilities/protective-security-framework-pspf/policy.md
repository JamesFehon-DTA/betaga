<p>Direct link:&nbsp;<a href="https://www.protectivesecurity.gov.au/"><em>protectivesecurity.gov.au</em></a>&nbsp;<br>Responsible agency:&nbsp;<strong>Department of Home Affairs</strong>&nbsp;<br>Last updated:&nbsp;<strong>December 2024</strong>&nbsp;</p><p>The <a href="https://www.protectivesecurity.gov.au/pspf-annual-release"><em>Protective security policy framework</em></a> prescribes what Australian Government entities must do to protect their people, information and resources, both domestically and internationally.&nbsp;</p><p>Application of the PSPF assures government that entities are:</p><ul><li>implementing sound and responsible protective security practices</li><li>identifying and mitigating security risks and vulnerabilities.&nbsp;</li></ul><p>The PSPF is reviewed annually to ensure it reflects the current threat environment. Entities are consulted on proposed updates via the Government Security Committee. Updates culminate in an annual release.&nbsp;</p><h2>Applicability&nbsp;</h2><p>Non-corporate Commonwealth entities that are subject to the <em>Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013</em> must apply the PSPF (to the extent consistent with legislation).&nbsp;</p><p>The PSPF provides direction and guidance for:</p><ul><li>The Accountable Authorities of Australian Government entities, per the&nbsp;<em>Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013</em>&nbsp;(PGPA Act).</li><li>Entity Chief Security Officers, Chief Information Security Officers, security practitioners and other named security officials.</li><li>Service providers that provide services to Australian Government entities or are required to implement the PSPF according to relevant deeds or agreements.</li><li>Those responsible for communicating security information to Australian Public Service (APS) employees, third-party service providers delivering services to Australian Government entities, and visitors to government facilities.</li><li>Those working within, and for, the Australian Government, including APS employees, third-party service providers and contracted staff.</li></ul><h2>Access the&nbsp;policy&nbsp;</h2><p>The <em>Protective security policy framework</em> website hosts the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.protectivesecurity.gov.au/publications-library/pspf-annual-release"><em>PSPF annual release</em></a>&nbsp;(full text).&nbsp;</p>
