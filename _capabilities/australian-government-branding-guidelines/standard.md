<p>Direct link: <a href="https://pmc.gov.au/resources/australian-government-branding-guidelines"><em>pmc.gov.au/resources/australian-government-branding-guidelines</em></a><br>Responsible agency: <strong>Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet</strong><br>Last updated: <strong>December 2024</strong></p><p>These guidelines have been developed to help departments and agencies ensure the Australian Government logo is consistently applied to products such as official documents and publications.</p><p>The Australian government branding guidelines include rules and guidance in the application of the Australian Government logo in relation to:</p><ul><li>design</li><li>colour</li><li>positioning</li><li>inclusion of other departments / agencies / programs</li><li>stationary logo application</li><li>website logo application </li><li>use of the logo by third parties&nbsp;</li></ul><h2>Applicability</h2><p>Departments and agencies are required to comply with the branding policy unless an exemption, or permission to use a co-branded logo, has been granted by the minister responsible for the branding policy.&nbsp;</p><p>Requests for an exemption or a co-branded logo are considered on a case-by-case basis.</p><h2>Access the guidelines&nbsp;</h2><p>The pmc.gov.au website hosts the <a href="https://www.pmc.gov.au/resources/australian-government-branding-guidelines">Australian Government branding guidelines standard</a> (full text).&nbsp;</p>
