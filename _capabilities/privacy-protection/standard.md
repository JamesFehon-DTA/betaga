<p>The Australian Government ensures the safety and security of its operations to remain a trusted custodian of sensitive information. This standard is designed to ensure that personal information of individuals is handled in a manner that is consistent with legislative and regulatory frameworks. This helps enhance trust in government, promote transparency, and foster a culture of privacy awareness.</p><p>It is critical this standard be considered alongside those of related capabilities:</p><ul><li>Application Security</li><li>Information Asset Security</li><li>Network Security</li><li>Permissions</li></ul><h2>Comply with legislation</h2><p>Entities must:</p><ul><li>comply with relevant Commonwealth legislation including (but not limited to):<ul><li><em>Privacy Act 1988</em> (Cth)<em>&nbsp;</em></li><li><em>Archives Act 1983</em> (Cth)</li><li><em>Freedom of Information Act 1982</em> (Cth)</li></ul></li><li>adhere to the principles-based law of the <a href="https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/australian-privacy-principles">Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)</a></li><li>comply with any other legislation applicable to specific functions and circumstances.</li></ul><p>Entities should:</p><ul><li>utilise the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) <a href="https://www.oaic.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0009/1125/app-guidelines-july-2019.pdf">APP guidelines</a> to assist in applying the APPs</li><li>seek independent legal advice where appropriate.</li></ul><h2>Align to guidelines and standards</h2><p>Entities must:</p><ul><li>apply the principles and requirements of the <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/protective-security-policy-framework-pspf">Protective Security Policy Framework</a> throughout their organisation.</li></ul><p>Entities should:</p><ul><li>participate in the <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/mygovid">Australian Government Digital Identity System</a></li><li>meet the requirements set out in the <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/trusted-digital-identity-framework-tdif-0">Trusted Digital Identity Framework</a></li><li>apply a risk-based approach to privacy to align with the <a href="https://www.cyber.gov.au/resources-business-and-government/essential-cyber-security/ism">Information Security Manual</a> (ISM)</li><li>be aware of, and apply, other standards relevant to their operating context.</li></ul><h2>Be private-by-design</h2><p>Entities should:</p><ul><li>embed privacy practices into the design and implementation of solutions</li><li>develop and maintain robust governance and reporting</li><li>ensure protocols, procedures, and technology solutions adapt to changing privacy laws, technologies, opportunities, and threats</li><li>foster a culture of privacy awareness through training and governance</li><li>follow OAIC’s <a href="https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/privacy-guidance-for-organisations-and-government-agencies/privacy-impact-assessments/privacy-by-design">Privacy by Design</a> guidance.</li></ul><h2>Take a proactive approach to privacy risks and incident responses</h2><p>Entities must:</p><ul><li>undertake a Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) for all high privacy risk projects</li><li>periodically review PIAs to reflect changes in technology and data usage</li><li>implement mechanisms to detect, report, and respond to data breaches in line with the <a href="https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/notifiable-data-breaches/about-the-notifiable-data-breaches-scheme">Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme</a>.</li></ul><p>Entities should:</p><ul><li>conduct PIAs even when not mandatory, to understand privacy impacts</li><li>use <a href="https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/privacy-guidance-for-organisations-and-government-agencies/privacy-impact-assessments/guide-to-undertaking-privacy-impact-assessments">the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner’s (OAIC’s) PIA guide</a> to tailor the PIA process to specific business needs</li><li>choose technology solutions with advanced monitoring and alerting capabilities</li><li>use the <a href="https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/privacy-guidance-for-organisations-and-government-agencies/preventing-preparing-for-and-responding-to-data-breaches/data-breach-preparation-and-response">OAIC’s data breach preparation and response</a> guide to prepare for, and respond to, a data breach.</li></ul><h2>Adhere to reuse principles</h2><p>The Australian Government Architecture provides information for entities on <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/reuse-standard">Reuse</a>.</p><p>Entities should:</p><ul><li>compare their requirements with those of other comparable entities and system functions, and seek to reuse learnings from preceding implementations</li><li>consider specific functional and non-functional requirements prior to solution design or consideration of technology choice, including:<ul><li>volume and nature of information assets</li><li>broader system purpose</li><li>performance and availability requirements</li><li>privacy/sensitivity concerns</li></ul></li><li>meet the requirements of the <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/digital-and-ict-reuse-policy">Digital and ICT Reuse Policy</a>.</li></ul>
