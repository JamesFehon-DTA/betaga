<p>This standard supports entities to adopt eInvoicing, improving internal processing, supporting small businesses, and reducing administrative delays and payment issues.</p><h2>Meet legal, regulatory, and policy requirements</h2><p>Entities must:</p><ul><li>comply with relevant Commonwealth legislation including (but not limited to): <ul><li><em>Archives Act 1983</em> (Cth)</li><li><em>Data Availability and Transparency (DAT) Act 2002</em> (Cth)</li><li><em>Payment Time Reporting Act 2020</em> (Cth)</li><li><em>Privacy Act 1988</em> (Cth)</li><li><em>Tax Administration Act 1953</em> (Cth)</li></ul></li><li>meet Supplier Pay on Time or Pay Interest Policy (RMG417) requirements</li><li>apply the principles and requirements of the <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/protective-security-policy-framework-pspf">Protective Security Policy Framework</a> throughout their organisation, including in the context of eInvoicing</li><li>comply with any other legislation applicable to specific functions and circumstances.</li></ul><h2>Be able to systemically receive Peppol-compliant eInvoices</h2><p>Entities must:</p><ul><li>be able to receive Peppol-compliant eInvoices as defined by the ATO as the Australian Peppol authority</li><li>utilise a valid <a href="https://github.com/A-NZ-PEPPOL/A-NZ-PEPPOL-BIS-3.0">A-NZ Peppol BIS 3.0 Invoice</a> through an <a href="https://softwaredevelopers.ato.gov.au/accreditation-Peppol-service-provider-register">accredited Australian or New Zealand Peppol Service Provider</a>.</li></ul><h2>Promote supplier take-up of eInvoicing</h2><p>Entities should:</p><ul><li>promote the participation of suppliers to utilise eInvoicing</li><li>encourage and support trading partners to onboard.</li></ul><h2>Adopt the full functionality of eInvoicing</h2><p>Entities must:</p><ul><li>enable eInvoicing functionality throughout applicable spaces of their operations</li><li>integrate eInvoicing functionality with the Financial Management Information System of the organisation’s Enterprise Resource Planning solution – see <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/erp-finance-standard">Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) Finance Standard</a>.</li></ul><p>Entities should:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>utilise the ATO’s <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/einvoicing-government-guidance">eInvoicing for government guidance</a> to prepare for any eInvoicing investment, aligning to the steps of:</li></ul><ol><li>Identify how you currently process invoices</li><li>Find out about your eInvoicing options</li><li>Get ready for eInvoicing</li><li>Onboard your suppliers</li></ol><ul><li>access and utilise the ATO’s <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/einvoicing-government-guidance">eInvoicing for government guidance</a> when adopting eInvoicing and engaging with suppliers</li><li>utilise the Peppol framework, connecting to ’access points’ accredited by the <a href="https://www.ato.gov.au/businesses-and-organisations/einvoicing/peppol">ATO as the Australian Peppol Authority</a></li><li>participate in the ATO’s Communities of Practice and Networks through engagement with <a href="mailto:eInvoicing@ato.gov.au">eInvoicing@ato.gov.au</a>.</li></ul><h2>Adhere to reuse principles</h2><p>The Australian Government Architecture provides information for entities on <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/reuse-standard">Reuse</a>.</p><p>Entities should:</p><ul><li>meet the requirements of the <a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/digital-and-ict-reuse-policy">Digital and ICT reuse policy</a></li><li>source via the Digital Marketplace on <a href="https://www.buyict.gov.au/sp?id=marketplace_landing&amp;marketplace=cbb1ec15db617b0031d7413b3a96190b&amp;path=buying">BuyICT</a>, including taking advantage of whole-of-government arrangements.</li></ul>
