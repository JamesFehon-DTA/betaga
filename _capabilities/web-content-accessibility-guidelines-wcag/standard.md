<p>The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) provides a single shared standard for making web content more accessible.&nbsp;</p><p>WCAG forms a part of the Digital Service Standard v2.0.</p><p>The WCAG standard is applicable to all digital services. Meeting the standard is a requirement of the:</p><ul><li><a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/digital-service-standard-v2">Digital Service Standard</a></li><li><a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/digital-inclusion-standard">Digital Inclusion Standard</a></li><li><a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/digital-sourcing-consider-first-policy">Digital Sourcing Consider First Policy</a></li><li><a href="https://www.finance.gov.au/government/procurement/commonwealth-procurement-rules">Commonwealth Procurement Rules&nbsp;</a></li><li><a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/australian-government-style-manual-digital-edition-0">Australian Government Style Manual&nbsp;</a></li><li><a href="https://architecture.digital.gov.au/web-content-management-standard">Australian Government Architecture (AGA) Web Content Management standard</a></li></ul><p>All commonwealth entities have an obligation to ensure their digital services comply with the Disability Discrimination Act 1992.</p><h2>Access the standard</h2><p>The www.w3.org website hosts the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/">Web Content Accessibility Guidelines</a> (full text).&nbsp;</p>
