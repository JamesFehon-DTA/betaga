<p>Direct link:<em> </em><a href="https://digital.gov.au/investment/strategic-planning/digital-investment-plans-dips/digital-investment-plan-policy"><em>www.digital.gov.au/investment/strategic-planning/digital-investment-plans-dips/digital-investment-plan-policy</em></a><br>Responsible agency: <strong>Digital Transformation Agency</strong><br>Last updated: <strong>25 June 2025</strong></p><p>The Digital Investment Plan (DIP) Policy and accompanying guidebook establishes a consistent, comprehensive and long-term approach to digital investment planning across government. It enables the Digital Transformation Agency (DTA) to effectively aggregate a whole-of-government view of the digital investment landscape.&nbsp;</p><p>The policy was developed to address critical gaps in planning by establishing a structured approach to short, medium and long-term digital investment planning.&nbsp;</p><p>The policy:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>specifies a consistent approach and minimum standards for strategic, future-focused digital investment planning</li><li>supports agencies to align with the vision and missions of the Data and Digital Government Strategy and other strategic priorities.&nbsp;</li></ul><p>The guidebook:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>outlines minimum content requirements for each section of a DIP</li><li>includes templates and guidance for content development.</li></ul><h2>Applicability</h2><p>The policy applies to all non-corporate Commonwealth entities subject to the Digital and ICT Investment Oversight Framework (IOF).</p><h2>Access the policy</h2><p>The digital.gov.au website hosts the <a href="https://digital.gov.au/investment/strategic-planning/digital-investment-plans-dips/digital-investment-plan-policy">Digital Investment Plan (DIP) Policy</a>.</p>

## Policy requirements

### Requirement 1

Have a digital investment plan in place by 1 July 2025.

### Requirement 2

Designate a suitable responsible officer to approve the plan.

### Requirement 3

Submit an approved plan to the DTA at least once annually.

### Requirement 4

Address the following minimum content requirements outlined in the DIP Guidebook:

an agency overview, introducing its role and functions
a list of important current systems that the agency uses or provides
a digital outlook vision statement
a planning section containing the digital investment plan or a roadmap
enablers that underpin the plan's delivery
risks which might disrupt the plan's delivery.

### Requirement 5

Ensure that proposals, projects and ongoing digital initiatives assessed by the DTA as part of the IOF are aligned with their plan.

### Requirement 6

Ensure all internally funded digital initiatives that are critical to agency functions are outlined in their plan.

### Requirement 7

Align the plan with the agency's strategies and demonstrate contribution towards the Data and Digital Government Strategy.

### Requirement 8

Review and refine digital investment plans twice a year, in line with budget cycles.
