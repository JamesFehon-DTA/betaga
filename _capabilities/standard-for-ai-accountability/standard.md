<p>Direct link: <a href="https://www.digital.gov.au/ai/ai-in-government-policy/accountability"><em>www.digital.gov.au/ai/ai-in-government-policy/accountability</em></a><br>Responsible agency: <strong>Digital Transformation Agency</strong><br>Last updated: <strong>1 December 2025</strong></p><h2>About the standard</h2><p>The <a href="https://www.digital.gov.au/ai/ai-in-government-policy/accountability">Standard for accountability</a> sets out:</p><ul><li>the requirement for government agencies to designate an official, or officials, to be responsible for the implementation of the Policy for responsible use of AI in government in their agency</li><li>the requirement that each AI use case in scope of the policy has designated accountability</li><li>the key responsibilities of accountable officials and accountable use case owners</li><li>how to establish and share the internal use case register.</li></ul><h2>Applicability</h2><p>Non-corporate Commonwealth entities must meet this standard. Check the <a href="https://www.digital.gov.au/ai/ai-in-government-policy">Policy for the responsible use of AI in government</a> for details and exemptions.</p><h2>Access the standard</h2><p>Read the <a href="https://www.digital.gov.au/ai/ai-in-government-policy/accountability">Standard for accountability</a>.</p>
